The Problem

Software projects fail
before they start.

Most teams reach for a general-purpose chatbot when they need architectural guidance. But generic prompts produce generic answers — incomplete, surface-level, and disconnected from the real constraints of your project.

By the time you realize the architecture was wrong, you've already built on a broken foundation.

  • Wrong questions asked upfront
    Users don't know what they don't know. Critical architectural constraints get missed entirely, leading to wrong foundations.
  • No trade-off visibility
    Tech stack decisions are made without understanding cost, complexity, or scalability implications. No one explains what you're giving up.
  • Static, one-shot outputs
    Architecture documents get created once and rot. There's no workspace for iteration as requirements, budgets, and scope evolve.
