The Problem

Every bad project started with the wrong architecture.

Most teams pick a stack before they understand their real constraints. Scale, cost, real-time behaviour — these aren't implementation details. They're the decisions that determine whether your project survives contact with reality.

  • Wrong stack, wrong foundation
    Tech choices made on familiarity, not fit. The wrong database or infra pattern compounds quietly — until the cost to fix it exceeds the cost to rebuild.
  • Constraints surface too late
    Payment architecture, tenant isolation, real-time requirements — discovered mid-build. Each late constraint rewrites assumptions that were already coded in.
  • Architecture that can't evolve
    Static diagrams written at kickoff are obsolete by sprint three. Requirements shift. Budgets change. The architecture stays frozen on a whiteboard nobody trusts.
